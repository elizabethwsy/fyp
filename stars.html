Greetings Young Padawan

The universe has brought you here to NTU by no accident. An exciting journey lies ahead of you.

At the beginning of every semester, a war of intense magnitude wages across the island of Pulau NTU. A war of survival, every man for himself. Some leave the war victorious while others are left fighting over scraps.

This is STAR Wars.

Looking win the war? Seek and ye shall find. Coined by NTU students, STAR Wars is a course registration process in NTU where you battle with your peers to get the courses you want for a good timetable.

Let us begin.

What's the Meaning of This?

Before you embark on your journey, there are some fundamentals you might want to know.

Just so you know:
  • STARS: STudents Automated Registration System
  • STARS Planner allows you to plan your timetable and add courses
  • STARS allows you to submit your planned timetable for registration or make changes to your courses
  • Add/Drop: The first 2 weeks of school where you can register new courses, de-register courses or change the timeslot of your registered courses
  • CORE courses: Courses you must take for your major
  • Major PE(Prescribed Electives): Courses you’ll choose from your major for specialisation
  • GER-CORE: 1 AU online courses or pre-registered courses that cover the following: Communication Skills, Singapore Studies, Sustainability, Ethics, Enterprise and Innovation
  • GER-PE: Specific courses from 3 categories: Business & Management, Liberal Arts, and Science, Technology, and Society. You must take at least 1 module from each category; total GER-PEs to take depends on your major.
  • UE(Unrestricted Electives): Courses offered by any other major that you can take. In my opinion, UEs make university life interesting.

First Things First

NTU uses a first come first serve system for course registration. So, FASTEST FINGERS FIRST!
Before you charge to register your courses, here's an overview of what to expect.
As a freshman you won't experience STAR Wars in your first semester, but there's still ADD/DROP. During which, you are able to make changes to your given timetable.

If you're new!


If you're an existing student

The entire registration process comprises of 4 stages:

1. Planning
2. Main Course Registration
3. GERPE/ UE Allocation
4. Add/ Drop

If you have just matriculated, courses have already been registered for you. So fret not, enjoy it while you still can. You'll be directed to print out your timetable after you have matriculated. Then, just wait for Add/ Drop.

If you are an existing student, CONGRATS on surviving your sem! But here's where things are going to be different.

The above 4 stages will come into play. First things first, Check your personalised timetable for course registration (yours might differ from your friend's, trust me on this!).

Fail to Plan, Plan to Fail

Plan your timetable before your Course Registration timeslot so that on the day itself, all you have to do is click a button and hope for the best. Else, a frantic headless chicken ye shall be.

  1. Go to your STARS Planner. There, you will see 3 columns: Course Codes, Option, Course.
  2. Select 'Option' followed by the Courses you want. Or enter the Course codes in directly.
  3. Press Plan!

  4. Select your desired time slots for each course
  5. Save your plan. You can save a total of 3 plans
  6. Take note of your Exam Schedule and make sure they don’t clash! Red boxes in your timetable mean your schedules clash and you should probably find another tutorial timeslot or another module. (sorry man!)

Now you're all set and ready for battle. If you're in your very first semester, your core courses are pre-registered (meaning you can't drop any). So all you need to do is print your timetable after matriculating online and swap during Add/Drop for a better timetable.

May the Force be with Thee

Just before your assigned timeslot, log in to your STARS Planner. Get ready.
The moment you are allowed into the STARS portal, click "Add Course(s) to STARS" to submit your timetable.

You'll be able to preview the courses you are about to add. Confirm the action to register your courses in STARS.

Core courses(includes GER Core) and major PEs are assigned on the spot if there are vacancies available. GERPE/UE courses will be placed on waitlist. If you are unsuccessful in adding any of your courses, fret not. Access STARS directly to add these courses or use the STARS Planner again.

In STARS, you can:
1. Drop registered courses/ Add new courses
2. Change your tutorial time (i.e. index number)
3. Check course vacancies
4. Reclassify GERPE/UE
5. Swap classes with another student in the same course
6. Print/Check Courses Registered

You can check your status for the course at PRINT/CHECK COURSES REGISTERED.

Things to note:

When you drop a course or change index number, your place in the current class will be withdrawn, and the vacancy thus created will be released to other students.

Adding UE/GERPE: you would be asked to rank them from 1-5 (1 being the course you want to get the most and 5 being the least)

UE/GERPE added during course registration will be allocated based on a set of priority agreed with all Schools.

Priority is given to Final-Year Students since they have a shorter time to meet their graduation requirements although it is not guaranteed.

You will be allocated up to 2 courses, 1 UE 1 GERPE. An e-mail notification of your allocated courses will be sent before the start of the semester.

If you didn't manage to add your courses during the stipulated time, you can do so again on the same day from 5pm to 10pm. Otherwise, your second chance will be during Add/Drop.


Your Second Chance

Allocations for UE and GERPE are not on first come first serve basis (hence the waitlist). On the first night of Add/Drop, the system will allocate UE/GERPE prioritising senior year students first. Subsequently, at 10pm every night till end of Add/Drop, the system allocates courses to students randomly. If you're allocated courses on the last day, you can drop any of your courses on the next day

During this 2-week Add/ Drop period, you can assess whether you like the courses you have registered or not. If you don't, you can drop it and try another without any penalty. You can also swap timeslots for a better timetable or reclassify your UE/GERPE.

If you're unsuccessful in getting the UE/GERPE, you can submit a course appeal form (no guarantees you'll get it though!) and pray that the system works in your favour. You'll be informed before the end of Add/Drop

Once the period is over, you'll receive a confirmation email on all the courses you'll take in the coming semester. Check your final timetable here.You can no longer submit anymore appeals/requests

Just a Side Note

If you're taking level 1 language modules for UE (go try it's fun!), you need to declare if you have previously studied the subject.

If you're taking modules that need auditions (mainly NIE courses). They are usually conducted during the first lesson. NIE will submit the names for the selected students to OAS for registration. You'll be notified once registration is completed

Thinking of taking a minor? You can inform the school your intention and appeal for any prerequisite waiver. Email the UPO with the approved waiver. Your UEs will be used to fulfil the requirements of your chosen minor.

For smarties who are offered to pursue a second major and accelerated program, you can register during your STAR Wars period or otherwise during Add/drop.

Clear outstanding fees at least 1 week before your scheduled registration; otherwise, you will be denied access to STARS.
