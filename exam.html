If Grades Do Matter

Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA)

CGPA is the grade average of all courses (failed ones too) attempted by a student. Only letter graded courses are computed

There's no need to know how to calculate it but if you really want:

[Grade Point x AU for Course 1] +…+ [Grade Point x AU for Course X]

[Total AU attempted in all semesters so far]


Freshman GPA Exemption

Freshmen can exempt up to 6 letter-grade courses that failed on the first attempt (F grade) *T&C Apply

Academic Standing

Criteria for satisfactory academic standing:

    ​​​
  • Min CGPA of 2.00
  • Complete > 75% of normal AU workload

Students with poor results receive the following and subjected to performance review:

  • Warning – CGPA < 2.00 for any semester
  • Probation – CGPA < 2.00 the following semester
  • Termination – CGPA < 2.00 for 3 consecutive semesters / at the end of final semester of study. A letter of termination will be issued

Dean’s List

Dean’s List is awarded to top 5% of the cohort, having YGPA > 4.50 and attained the specified AU of graded courses by curriculum type taken in NTU in the academic year

Post-Exam Information

Hurray, you're done with your exams!
Here’s what you can do next:

1. Check Exam Results
2. Review Exam Results
3. Retrieve Lost Items


If you're not happy with your grades,

- Apply for an examination results review.
- All applications must be submitted online through StudentLink and payments made within 1 week from the date of release of examination results.

Login in HERE!

You will be charged $10.00 per course ($10.70 with GST). Make your payment online or at One Stop@SAC.


Appeal Against Outcome of Review

If you are dissatisfied with the outcome of the review, you may appeal to the Academic Appeal Board at the University level if you have sufficient grounds.


If you've left something in the exam hall,

Any items left behind inside the examination hall will be placed in a lost and found box inside the hall throughout the examination period. So you can go back to the hall 30 mins before the start of the next paper to get it.

For items left outside of the examination hall, proceed to One Stop@SAC the next working day.

When Absent From Exams

If you’re sick during exam:

1. Inform the Chief invigilator
2. See the doctor if you’re too ill to continue. (Your paper will still be marked but special consideration may be given if you submit a doctor's memo)

If you’re sick before the exam:

You need to see a doctor and submit:

1. Original MC via MC Submission Form
2. AAA Form


Absence

If you've registered for a course but fail to take the exam, you'll be considered to have sat for and failed the exam

1. Authorisation for Release of Information And Acknowledgement (AAA) Form

- Get this endorsed by a medical practitioner and also ensure the diagnosis is indicated on your MC.

2.  MC Submission Form

- Submit this online
- indicate the period of your MC and the affected exams within the MC period. Submit the Form with your original MC to One Stop @ SAC.

Tips and Advice

Helplines Just In Case

Before the exam:

1. List the things that you've finished studying and how much more is left to cover
2. Review Past Year Papers and Tutorials
3. Check your knowledge against Learning Objectives (make sure you've covered everything)
4. Check your exam format (know what you're up against)
5. Find your lecturers if you need clarifications
6. Get enough sleep!

Be sure to be at the exam venue on time (at least 10 mins before) so you have time to calm yourself before the start of the paper

On the Day of Your Exam

Make sure you've brought your:
- Matric Card
- Jacket
- Water Bottle
- Calculator/ Dictionary/ Texts
- Stationery
